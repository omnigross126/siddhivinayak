Question & Answers

If you're late returning the rental car, rental companies typically have a grace period (often 30 minutes to an hour) before charging extra fees. After that period, you may be charged for an additional day or incur late fees. To avoid penalties, it’s best to inform the rental company in advance if you anticipate being late.
In many cases, rental companies and tour operators allow for extensions if the schedule permits. However, it's crucial to contact the company as early as possible to check availability. Extending a car rental often involves adjusting the rental rate, while tours may need to be rescheduled depending on availability.
If the rental car breaks down, most companies provide roadside assistance as part of their service. The process usually involves contacting the rental company for support, which can include repair services or replacing the vehicle. It’s important to check what kind of roadside assistance is offered when booking.
It’s generally a good idea to book tours and car rentals in advance, especially during peak travel seasons. For popular destinations or limited tours, booking weeks or even months in advance can ensure availability. Last-minute bookings might be possible, but the selection may be more limited and prices may be higher.
Cancellation policies vary by company. For car rentals, most companies allow free cancellations up to 24–48 hours before the pickup time. For tours, the policy may depend on the type of tour—some offer free cancellation up to a few days before the tour, while others may have stricter rules. Be sure to check the specific terms when booking.
